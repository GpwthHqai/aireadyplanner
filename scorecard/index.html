Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Diagnostic work? ▼

We schedule a 2-hour session with your communications leadership team. During the session, we walk through 60+ targeted questions across 5 dimensions of AI readiness, plus workflow mapping and a technology audit. You receive your AI Readiness Score, a radar chart of your strengths and gaps, and a written report with prioritized recommendations within 48 hours.

What is the AI Comms Sprint? ▼

The AI Comms Sprint is a 4-week implementation engagement where we take the findings from your Diagnostic and build 1-2 custom AI-powered tools tailored to your team's exact workflows, then train your team to use them. We discuss Sprint scope and pricing during the strategy follow-up call after your Diagnostic.

Can we run this with multiple teams? ▼

Yes. Each Diagnostic engagement covers one communications team. If you have multiple teams or business units, we can scope a multi-team assessment at a custom rate.

Who should be in the room for the Diagnostic? ▼

Ideally, the VP or Director of Communications plus 1-2 senior team members who manage day-to-day content production. The assessment is most valuable when the people setting strategy and the people doing the work are both represented.

How is the Diagnostic different from the free assessment? ▼

The free assessment on this site is a quick self-serve scorecard that gives you a baseline across 5 dimensions. It's a great starting point. The Diagnostic goes much deeper: a guided 2-hour session with your leadership team covering 60+ questions, workflow mapping, and a technology audit. You walk away with a written report, a strategy call, and a concrete implementation roadmap.

What if we already use AI tools? ▼

Most teams we work with have adopted some AI tools. The Diagnostic measures how effectively those tools are integrated into your actual workflows, whether your team has the skills to use them well, and whether your governance framework is keeping pace. Having tools and using them strategically are two different things.
