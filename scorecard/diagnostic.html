AI Readiness Diagnostic

Intake Tool for Enterprise Assessment Sessions

Client Information

1 Phase 1: Context & Discovery

▶
1. What are the primary business goals for your organization over the next 12 months?
Understanding strategic priorities helps identify where AI can create competitive advantage. Look for revenue growth, market expansion, or operational efficiency goals.
2. How is your organization currently using AI or AI-adjacent tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, automation, etc.)?
Current AI adoption reveals the baseline maturity and identifies shadow AI use. This informs recommendations and risk areas.
3. What specific pain points or bottlenecks are slowing down your communications and content creation?
Pain points map directly to AI opportunities. This surfaces the "where to start" for recommendations.
4. Who are your primary audiences and what content types do you create for them?
Audience and content type inform the right AI applications and data governance model.
5. What's your content creation process from idea to publishing? How long does it typically take?
Process mapping reveals where AI can eliminate steps, reduce cycle time, and improve quality gates.
6. Who are the key stakeholders in content approval and decision-making?
Stakeholder mapping informs change management, governance structure, and adoption readiness.
7. How does your team currently measure content performance and ROI?
Existing measurement practices inform post-AI performance tracking and success metrics.
8. What communication channels do you prioritize? (email, website, social, internal, video, etc.)
Channel priorities determine which AI tools and applications should be prioritized first.
9. What are your biggest concerns about adopting AI in communications?
Concerns surface potential adoption barriers and the need for governance, training, and risk mitigation.
10. How do you currently manage brand voice and consistency across your communications?
Brand voice governance readiness determines whether AI-assisted content can maintain consistency or needs guardrails.
11. What's your biggest challenge with current team capacity and resource constraints?
Resource constraints are a primary driver for AI adoption. Quantifies the value of efficiency gains.
12. How aligned is your team on AI adoption, and what might be their concerns?
Team alignment is critical for successful AI implementation. Identifies training needs and change management strategy.
13. What does success look like for AI in your communications function over the next 12 months?
Success metrics drive the roadmap and measure impact of recommendations.
14. Are there any compliance, security, or governance requirements we need to be aware of?
Compliance requirements shape the governance model and tool selection for AI implementation.
15. What budget or resources are you prepared to invest in AI-driven improvements?
Budget constraints and ROI expectations guide recommendation scope and phasing.
16. Who owns the decisions about adopting new tools and processes? What's the approval timeline?
Decision-making authority and timelines inform the roadmap and engagement strategy with leadership.
17. Are there specific tools, platforms, or systems you're already invested in that we should integrate with?
Existing tool ecosystem determines which AI solutions can be integrated and which require replacement.
18. What would be your ideal first step or quick win with AI in the next 30-60 days?
Quick-win preferences inform the sprint scope and momentum-building recommendations.

2 Phase 2: Workflow Mapping

▶
1. Describe your main content workflows (e.g., blog posts, email campaigns, social content, thought leadership)
Workflow inventory helps identify which processes benefit most from AI acceleration.
2. For your primary content workflow, what are the key steps and bottlenecks?
Bottleneck identification is critical for AI opportunity prioritization.
3. How long does each stage typically take (research, drafting, editing, approval, publishing)?
Time allocation reveals where AI can deliver the biggest time savings.
4. Who's involved in each step and what's their role? (Creator, reviewer, approver, publisher)
Role clarity informs which teams need AI training and which AI tools serve which functions.
5. What quality standards or review criteria are applied before publishing?
Quality criteria determine guardrails needed in AI-generated content and review processes.
6. Where do you currently source research, data, or inspiration for content?
Research sourcing informs where AI can augment or accelerate discovery and synthesis.
7. What handoffs or communication challenges exist between stages or teams?
Handoff inefficiencies are prime targets for workflow automation and tool integration.
8. How do you repurpose or reuse content across channels or formats?
Repurposing practices reveal where AI can multiply content ROI and channel reach.
9. What templates, frameworks, or standardized approaches help your team stay efficient?
Existing frameworks can be enhanced or automated with AI, increasing throughput.
10. Are there any metrics or KPIs you track for each workflow? How do they inform improvements?
Existing workflow metrics provide baseline for measuring AI impact post-implementation.
11. How often do workflows change, and what triggers updates or process improvements?
Process change frequency reveals organizational agility and receptiveness to AI-enabled workflows.
12. Of all your content workflows, which one should we prioritize for AI integration first?
Priority workflow selection informs the sprint scope recommendation and first-implementation roadmap.

3 Phase 3: Technology Audit

▶
1. What tools do you currently use for content creation, management, and distribution?
Current tech stack reveals integration opportunities and potential AI tool conflicts.
2. Are there any AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Jasper, Copy.ai, etc.) your team is already using informally?
Shadow AI reveals market demand and can be the foundation for formal governance and standardization.
3. How satisfied is your team with your current toolset? What gaps exist?
Tool satisfaction and gaps inform whether AI solutions should replace, augment, or integrate with existing systems.
4. Are your tools well-integrated, or do you manually move data between systems?
Integration gaps represent both risks and opportunities for AI-powered workflow automation.
5. Do you have any APIs or automation (Zapier, Make, etc.) connecting your tools?
Existing automation infrastructure can extend to include AI-powered processes and guardrails.
6. What's your data governance or security model? Any sensitive content considerations?
Data governance determines which AI tools are permissible and what guardrails are needed.
7. Are there IT, security, or compliance policies that limit which tools you can adopt?
Policy constraints shape the feasible AI solution set and approval pathway.
8. How do you currently handle version control, asset management, and content archiving?
Asset management maturity affects readiness for AI-powered content generation and knowledge management.
9. Do you have a knowledge base, internal wiki, or centralized content repository?
Knowledge base availability enables AI-powered retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for branded content.
10. What's your IT infrastructure and budget for new tools or integrations?
Infrastructure and budget constraints determine implementation scope and tool selection.

4 Phase 4: AI Readiness Deep Dive

▶

Dimension 1: Strategy & Vision

1. How well-defined is your organization's AI strategy? Is it documented and communicated?
Clear AI strategy enables focused implementation and cross-functional buy-in.
2. What are the specific, measurable outcomes you hope to achieve with AI?
Measurable outcomes drive prioritization, ROI tracking, and success criteria.
3. How is leadership committed to supporting AI adoption? What resources are they willing to invest?
Leadership commitment is essential for overcoming adoption barriers and sustaining change.

Dimension 2: Tools & Infrastructure

4. How would you rate your organization's current technology infrastructure readiness for AI?
Infrastructure readiness determines implementation complexity and tool selection strategy.
5. What integration points are most critical for AI tools in your workflows?
Critical integration points inform tool selection and implementation complexity.
6. Do you have the technical expertise in-house to implement and maintain AI tools, or will you need external support?
Skill assessment determines training needs and the case for implementation support or managed services.

Dimension 3: Skills & Training

7. How would you rate your team's current AI literacy and comfort with using AI tools?
Current AI literacy informs training approach, change management strategy, and early adopter selection.
8. What specific skills or training does your team need to effectively use AI tools?
Skill gaps inform training curriculum and change management approach.
9. How would your team respond to a structured training program on AI? What format would work best?
Training preferences ensure adoption program resonates with team culture and learning styles.

Dimension 4: Governance & Risk

10. Does your organization have any current AI governance policies or use guidelines?
Existing governance provides a foundation for expanded AI policy and risk management.
11. What are your primary concerns regarding AI risks (bias, accuracy, data privacy, liability)?
Risk concerns drive governance requirements, tool selection, and implementation safeguards.
12. Who should own AI governance and decision-making—Marketing, IT, Legal, Executive leadership?
Clear governance ownership ensures accountability and alignment with organizational values.

Dimension 5: Change Management & Culture

13. How does your organization typically respond to major process or technology changes?
Past change patterns inform change management strategy and implementation sequencing.
14. What concerns about job displacement or team morale exist around AI adoption?
Addressing these concerns early prevents implementation barriers and supports adoption.
15. How can we frame AI adoption as an opportunity for your team to do more strategic work?
Reframing AI as an opportunity maximizes engagement and supports a positive adoption narrative.

5 Phase 5: Prioritization & Roadmap

▶
1. Which content type or workflow would have the highest ROI with AI implementation?
ROI assessment drives prioritization of first-phase implementations and resource allocation.
2. What's your timeline for implementing AI solutions? Do you prefer a sprint or phased approach?
Timeline and approach preferences determine roadmap structure and sprint scope.
3. Are there any quick wins or low-hanging fruit we should tackle first to build momentum?
Quick wins build credibility and momentum for broader AI adoption initiatives.
4. What metrics or milestones would define success for your first AI project?
Clear success metrics enable objective evaluation and course correction during implementation.
5. Beyond the initial sprint, what's your vision for AI transformation over 6-12 months?
Longer-term vision ensures roadmap aligns with strategic goals beyond the first project.
6. What dependencies, blockers, or prerequisites need to be addressed before moving forward?
Identifying blockers upfront enables proactive mitigation and realistic timelines.
7. Who should be the champion or executive sponsor for this AI initiative?
Executive sponsorship is critical for overcoming resistance and securing necessary resources.
8. What's your next step after this diagnostic? (Sprint with Vernon, internal planning, proposal review, etc.)
Clear next steps ensure momentum and accountability following the diagnostic.
